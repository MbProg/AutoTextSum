U.S President William Mckinley Was Assassinated In Buffalo, 1901

 

 

The 25th President of the United States, William McKinley was shot and fatally wounded on September 6, 1901, inside the Temple of Music on the grounds of the Pan-American Exposition in Buffalo, New York. McKinley was shaking hands with the public when he was shot twice by a man named Leon Czolgosz. One bullet grazed the President; the other entered his abdomen and was never found. The President died on September 14 from gangrene caused by the bullet wounds.
