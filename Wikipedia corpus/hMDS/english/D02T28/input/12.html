Title: Constitutional Alcalde of the Jurisdiction of Austin
Location/Date: San Felipe de Austin: December 7, 1832
Collection: Texas Memorial Museum Collection
Constitutional Alcalde of the Jurisdiction of Austin
Held in San Felipe de Austin, the Convention of 1832 adopted a series of resolutions to be presented to the Mexican Congress. One of the resolutions called for organizing a militia and committees of vigilance, safety, and correspondence. Though Texas never presented the resolutions to the Congress, some of the resolutions held firm. This certificate confirms Edward Burleson as a lieutenant colonel in a provisional militia.

Title: Memorial to the Congress of the United Mexican States [English]
Date: April, 13, 1833
Collection: Edward Eberstadt Collection
Memorial to the Congress of the United Mexican States [English]
The Convention of 1833 met at San Felipe de Austin in April 1833. Among other things, the convention petitioned for the separation of Coahuila and Texas into two separate states and prepared a constitution for the state of Texas. The convention appointed a committee to write a memorial to the Mexican government outlining the virtues of the new state organization and constitution. Stephen F. Austin traveled to Mexico City to present the memorial to the Mexican government.
